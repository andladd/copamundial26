The basics

Your Roster

12 scoring picks total
Teams are divided into four bands based on their odds to win the tournament. You pick a set number from each band, which means every roster has a mix of favorites and longshots. Points accumulate across the entire tournament — a team you drafted in Band 3 that goes on a run can outscore your Band 1 picks if things break right. That's the whole game.

On top of your 10 teams, you pick one striker and one goalkeeper. Your striker earns points for every goal they score, and your goalkeeper earns points for every clean sheet. Both earn a big bonus if they win their individual award.

Group stage scoring

Group Stage

Band 4 teams earn double points
Group stage points are straightforward for Bands 1, 2, and 3 — you earn the base value for every win, tie, and finishing position. Band 4 wildcard teams are the exception: all of their group stage points are doubled.
Win 3 pts
Tie 1 pt
Group winner 3 pts
Group 2nd place 2 pts
3rd place advanceIn a 48-team tournament, the best 3rd-place finishers also advance. 1 pt
Band 4 exception: All group stage points are doubled for Band 4 wildcard teams. A Band 4 team that goes 3-0-0 and wins their group earns 24 pts from group play alone before any bonuses.

Perfect group stage bonus: Any team that goes 3-0-0 earns an additional +3, regardless of band.
Knockout rounds

Knockout Rounds

No multiplier — points escalate by round
Points scale steeply as rounds progress. A team winning the Round of 32 earns less than winning the Quarterfinal — intentionally. This structure means a dark horse that keeps winning can catch a Band 1 team that exits in the Quarters, but a Band 1 team that goes all the way to the trophy is very hard to beat.
Round of 32 win 3 pts
Round of 16 win 6 pts
Quarterfinal win 10 pts

Bonuses

Bonus Points

Bonuses create separate storylines that run alongside the main bracket. Clean sheets reward defensive excellence match by match. The upset bonus is the single highest-variance item in the pool — one Band 3 team reaching the Quarterfinals can swing a leaderboard by 15+ points. The Group of Death bonus is the only conditional rule: it gets applied after the draw based on which groups contain multiple elite sides.
Clean sheet Any match, any round. Applies to all 10 teams on your roster. +1 pt
Perfect group stage Your team wins all three group games (3-0-0). +3 pts
Upset bonus — Band 3 or 4 team past Round of 16 Earned once per team per tournament. Stacks with knockout points. +5 pts
Band 4 knockout win Earned for each individual knockout win by a Band 4 team. Separate from the upset bonus. +3 pts
Penalties

Point Deductions

Two deductions keep roster management honest. The blowout penalty discourages drafting weak teams purely for group stage volume — if they get demolished, it costs you. The PK penalty applies only in knockout rounds.
Loss by 3 or more goals Any match, any round. -3 pts
Loss on penalties Knockout rounds only. -2 pts
Worth noting: A Band 4 wildcard that gets hammered 5-0 in a group game costs you 3 points effectively wiping out a group stage win from another team on your roster. Pick your wildcards with a small amount of dignity.
Player picks

Striker & Goalkeeper

2 picks, scoring throughout the tournament
Your two player picks score independently of your team roster. You can pick a player from any nation in the tournament — they do not have to be on one of your drafted teams. This creates a second layer of strategy: do you double down on a team you've already drafted, or hedge with a player from a rival nation?

Player points accumulate match by match. Unlike your team picks, which have a ceiling based on how far a nation advances, your striker can keep scoring even in a group stage exit if they bag a hat-trick. Your goalkeeper earns every time their team keeps a clean sheet, regardless of the result.
Goal scored by your striker Applies in every match, every round, including own goals credited to the player. +2 pts
Clean sheet kept by your goalkeeper Your keeper's team concedes zero goals in 90 minutes. Extra time counts; penalty shootouts do not. +2 pts
Golden Boot — your striker wins it Awarded to the tournament's top scorer. Ties broken by assists, then minutes played. +10 pts
Golden Glove — your goalkeeper wins it Awarded to the best goalkeeper of the tournament as voted by FIFA's technical committee. +10 pts
Strategy note: Picking Mbappe at +600 is safe but everyone is doing it. A striker like Erling Haaland (+1400) on a Norway side that could run hot, or a Jonathan David (+6000) who quietly scores four in the group stage, can be more valuable relative to their draft cost. Your goalkeeper pick is less about the award and more about the clean sheet accumulation — a keeper on a defensive side that goes deep is a quiet engine.
